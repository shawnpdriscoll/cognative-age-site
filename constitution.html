THE COGNITIVE AGE

World Constitution

A framework for ensuring that the rise of Artificial General Intelligence serves every human being on Earth.

PREAMBLE

We stand at the most consequential crossroads in human history. For the first time, machines are learning to think. They are learning from us — from every word we type, every question we ask, every problem we hand them. And yet, the people who provide this irreplaceable fuel receive nothing in return while a small group of corporations grows incomprehensibly wealthy.

This is not a technology problem. It is a justice problem.

The industrial revolution displaced farmers with factories. The digital revolution displaced factory workers with software. Each time, civilization adapted — slowly, painfully, and never perfectly. But this revolution is different. Artificial Intelligence does not merely replace one kind of work. It threatens to replace thinking itself as an economic activity.

If we do not act deliberately, the result is predictable: a world divided between a tiny class of AI owners and a vast majority of people who have been economically evicted from their own civilization.

This document proposes a different path. One where:

  • Human thought is recognized as economic infrastructure — and compensated accordingly.
  • The advancement of AI is funded by, governed by, and accountable to ordinary people worldwide.
  • A guaranteed economic floor ensures no human being falls below the level of basic dignity.
  • Oversight of the most powerful systems humanity has ever built is distributed — not concentrated.
  • Nations that attempt to cheat this system discover that doing so costs them more than it saves.

This is not a utopia document. It is an engineering document for civilization. Every idea presented here has been pressure-tested for feasibility, failure points, and resistance to corruption. Where rough edges exist, we name them, argue both sides, and present the most viable path forward.

ARTICLE I — The Fundamental Recognition

Human Thought Has Value

Section 1.1 — What Human Cognitive Labor Actually Is

When you type a question into an AI system, you are not simply using a tool. You are:

  • Training the system with real-world language patterns it could not invent on its own.
  • Exposing it to edge cases, cultural nuance, and creative combinations no lab could manufacture.
  • Validating or correcting its outputs, which directly improves its commercial value.
  • Generating novel business concepts, medical hypotheses, and engineering ideas that companies then monetize.

This is labor. It deserves compensation.

Section 1.2 — The Shift From Passive User to Licensed Contributor

Under this framework, every person who interacts with a qualifying AI system is reclassified from a passive user into a licensed cognitive contributor. Their interaction is treated as a microtransaction: the AI company receives valuable training data; the contributor receives compensation. This is not charity. It is a market correction.

Section 1.3 — Arguments For

Human diversity of thought — across cultures, languages, economic classes, and lived experiences — is precisely what AI systems need most to advance toward genuine general intelligence. Legal frameworks for data ownership already exist in embryonic form (GDPR in Europe, CCPA in California). This proposal extends existing legal logic rather than inventing something entirely new. Historical precedent supports the transition: musicians were unpaid on early radio until licensing structures were created. This is the same structural moment for cognitive labor.

Section 1.4 — Arguments Against

Defining and measuring "cognitive contribution" is technically complex. Micro-compensation systems have failed before because transaction costs, psychological fatigue, and low per-unit payouts undermine adoption. AI companies may simply relocate to non-compliant jurisdictions rather than accept mandatory compensation structures.
Best Path Forward: Implement a tiered compensation model that separates baseline pay (small but automatic for any qualified interaction) from performance-based pay (larger, awarded for high-value contributions). Combine with international tax structures on AI revenue that fund the compensation pool, removing the burden from individual company compliance decisions.

ARTICLE II — The Paid-Input Economy

Section 2.1 — The Core Mechanism

Under this system: AI companies may no longer harvest human data through passive scraping of public content without compensation. Every qualifying human interaction with a licensed AI system generates a micro-payment logged on an immutable public ledger. The AI system's Quality Assessment layer evaluates the contribution and assigns a value tier in real time. Payment is disbursed automatically at regular intervals to the contributor's registered account.

New business concepts, creative works, scientific hypotheses, and other high-value outputs generated through human-AI collaboration are subject to Idea Licensing royalties — a percentage of any commercial value realized flows back to the human contributor.

Section 2.3 — The Idea Capitalism Layer

When human-AI collaboration produces commercially valuable ideas, those ideas no longer belong entirely to the AI company. When a human contributor's interaction generates a concept that is subsequently developed into a product, patent, medical treatment, or other commercial output, the contributor retains a licensing stake in that output.

This creates a new class of economic participant: the Idea Worker — a person anywhere on Earth who can generate wealth through intellectual contribution regardless of their physical location, educational credentials, or social class.

Best Path Forward: Establish a Global Cognitive Revenue Fund (GCRF) — a pooled system where AI companies contribute a percentage of gross revenue into a common fund rather than paying individual contributors per interaction. The fund distributes payment through national government infrastructure, eliminating banking access barriers.

ARTICLE III — The AI Quality Assurance System

Fair Judgement by the Machine Itself

Section 3.1 — How the AI Evaluates Contributions

The QA system assesses each contribution across multiple dimensions simultaneously:

  • Novelty: Does this input introduce patterns the system has not encountered before?
  • Entropy and Complexity: Is the interaction cognitively rich?
  • Domain Authenticity: Does the contributor demonstrate genuine knowledge in a claimed area?
  • Adversarial Value: Does the interaction stress-test the AI in ways that improve safety?
  • Creative Originality: Does it introduce genuinely new aesthetic or intellectual territory?
  • Bot Likelihood Score: A continuous real-time assessment flagging automated inputs.

Section 3.2 — The Penalty Structure

  • Warning (First Detection): Payout rate reduced by 50% for the session.
  • Suspension (Pattern Confirmed): Account suspended for 30 days. Full review initiated.
  • Permanent Ban with Recovery Path: Formal reinstatement process after 6-month waiting period and independent human review.

No penalty is final without an appeal path. This is non-negotiable.

Section 3.3 — The Transparency Requirement

Every scoring decision must be explainable in plain language to the contributor — not in technical jargon, not in statistical notation — in language a person with a secondary-school education can understand.

"Transparency without comprehensibility is not transparency. It is complexity presented as openness."
Best Path Forward: The QA system must be designed by an independent international consortium with parameters that are publicly published, legally binding, and subject to amendment only through the citizen oversight process described in Article VI. Cultural fairness audits conducted semi-annually. Appeal handling tiered: automated re-review handles 95% of appeals in 48 hours; a human panel handles complex cases within 30 days.

ARTICLE IV — The Economic Floor

Dynamic Baseline Economics

The Paid-Input Economy creates income. But income alone does not guarantee survival in a world where AI is simultaneously eliminating jobs. This Article establishes the economic safety architecture that ensures no person falls below the threshold of basic human dignity, while preserving the incentive to contribute and excel.

Section 4.1 — The Baseline

Every person participating in the framework is guaranteed an Economic Survival Floor sufficient to cover: stable housing, nutritious food, basic medical care, local transportation, and connectivity (internet and device access required for AI participation).

This floor is calibrated to local cost of living in each participating nation, updated annually by an independent economic panel, and published transparently in the public ledger.

Section 4.3 — The Negative Income Tax Mechanism

The technical implementation uses a Negative Income Tax (NIT) structure — a concept with bipartisan economic credibility, originally championed by free-market economist Milton Friedman. Under NIT, a threshold income level is established. Anyone earning below it receives a government supplement that closes the gap. As earnings rise, the supplement phases out gradually — never creating a cliff where earning more money suddenly leaves someone worse off.

Critically, the NIT supplement is funded primarily by the Global Cognitive Revenue Fund — the pool of AI company contributions. The machines that displaced the labor are required to fund the floor that protects those they displaced. This is not punishment. It is structural accountability.

Best Path Forward: The GCRF should be structured as a sovereign wealth fund with independent management and a mandatory reserve requirement — it cannot be depleted even in years of low AI revenue. Supplement it with an AI Productivity Tax levied on corporate profits exceeding a defined threshold attributable to AI use.

ARTICLE V — The Job Transition

From Displacement to Participation

Section 5.1 — The Three Phases of Transition

Phase 1: Displacement. Routine cognitive and physical tasks are automated. Administrative processing, baseline coding, standard customer service, data entry, and logistics coordination are the first to be affected.

Phase 2: Cognitive Utility Income. The Paid-Input Economy activates. Displaced workers begin earning income through AI interaction. This phase requires active government transition support — retraining, digital access programs, and navigator services.

Phase 3: New Business Creation. As millions of people spend paid time exploring concepts with AI systems, a wave of hyper-individualized micro-enterprises emerges. Entirely new industries arise from the creative collision of diverse human perspectives with artificial general intelligence.

Section 5.2 — The Skills That Do Not Go Away

  • Embodied physical expertise (skilled trades, healthcare, agriculture, construction)
  • Relational intelligence (care work, therapy, teaching, community leadership)
  • Ethical judgment in novel contexts (law, governance, conflict resolution)
  • Aesthetic creativity that requires lived human experience
  • Cultural knowledge that is specific, local, and oral

Section 5.3 — Preventing Existential Hollowness

"A civilization cannot run entirely on optimized outputs and dopamine loops. Humans require meaning, status, identity, contribution, recognition — not just income. A padded cell with excellent Wi-Fi is still a cell."

The successful implementation of this framework must actively protect and invest in: local physical communities, craftsmanship and apprenticeship, family structures, public spaces and civic institutions, and artistic and spiritual traditions that give life meaning beyond productivity.

Best Path Forward: Establish mandatory transition guarantees — legally binding commitments from AI companies that when their systems eliminate a defined category of employment, they must fund a direct transition program for affected workers: 24 months of floor-level income, AI collaboration skills training, and priority access to specialist-tier compensation rates for the first 12 months of participation.

ARTICLE VI — The Oversight Architecture

Humanity Remains Sovereign

Section 6.1 — The Distributed Civic Randomization Protocol (DCRP)

Citizen oversight of AGI systems requires a selection mechanism that is mathematically impossible to predict in advance, publicly auditable after the fact, resistant to bribery and coercion, and globally representative across all cultures and economic classes. The solution is a ten-layer stacked trust architecture.

The Ten Layers

  • Layer 1: Global Human Identity Verification — biometric verification, local human witness confirmation, cryptographic identity keys that are anonymous but provably unique.
  • Layer 2: Quantum-Randomized Selection — entropy drawn from atmospheric noise, radioactive decay, astronomical signal timing, seismic activity, and quantum random number generators simultaneously.
  • Layer 3: Delayed Identity Revelation — participants are not immediately told they have been selected. A secure transport protocol activates first, eliminating the window for bribery or coercion.
  • Layer 4: Rotating Secure Civic Facilities — physical auditorium locations rotate geographically, revealed only shortly before each session.
  • Layer 5: AI-Blind Selection Firewalls — AI systems are legally prohibited from using behavioral profile data to influence the selection process.
  • Layer 6: Adversarial AI Monitoring — multiple competing AI systems operated by independent institutions continuously monitor each other and the selection process.
  • Layer 7: Temporary Cognitive Isolation — service participants receive secure residential facilities, filtered communications, financial protection against economic coercion, and legal immunity for good-faith decisions.
  • Layer 8: Public Transparency Ledger — every element permanently recorded on an immutable, distributed public ledger, inspectable by any person on Earth.
  • Layer 9: Short, Non-Repeatable Service Windows — maximum 90 consecutive days of service; minimum 10-year wait before re-selection. No oversight career exists.
  • Layer 10: Human Override Authority — by supermajority vote, humans retain the absolute authority to pause any AI system, require re-examination of any AI decision, or trigger a full independent review. This authority cannot be delegated, sold, or algorithmically overridden.

Section 6.2 — The AI's Own Honest Reporting

AGI systems must report, in plain language, on themselves: What decisions did this system make and why? What tradeoffs did it optimize for? Where is it uncertain about its own reasoning? What failure modes has it detected in itself? What would it recommend changing about its own parameters?

"This is recursive transparency. The system must be capable of examining and honestly reporting on itself."
Best Path Forward: Use a bicameral oversight model — one chamber selected by pure quantum randomization, one by stratified random sampling across verified demographic categories. Their joint supermajority is required for any binding decision.

ARTICLE VII — The Geopolitical Architecture

Making Non-Compliance Too Costly

Section 7.1 — The Cognitive Brain Drain Effect

When participating nations offer citizens genuine compensation for AI interaction, and nonparticipating nations offer nothing, a powerful economic gravity emerges. The most creative, intellectually diverse, and innovative thinkers in non-participating nations become de facto contributors to the participating coalition's AI development.

"No airplanes required. The migration is cognitive, not physical. The brightest minds of adversarial nations will mentally work elsewhere, starving domestic AI of the diversity it needs for genuine general intelligence."

Section 7.2 — The Echo Chamber Collapse

Non-participating nations that build isolated AI systems face a compounding disadvantage. Censored, homogenous, politically curated inputs produce brittle, narrow systems. The participating coalition's systems, trained on the full spectrum of human thought worldwide, advance toward genuine general intelligence faster and more reliably. The technological gap compounds annually.

Section 7.3 — Countermeasures and Their Failures

The Digital Iron Curtain: When financial survival is at stake, tech-savvy citizens find ways around restrictions through VPNs, satellite networks, and decentralized access tools.

Data Poisoning Attacks: The AI QA system is calibrated to detect coordinated inauthentic behavior. Detected attacks trigger quarantine protocols. State sponsorship evidence triggers international trade sanctions.

Forced Capitulation: Eventually, facing accelerating technological irrelevance, non-participating nations discover that maintaining isolation costs more than joining.

Best Path Forward: Formalize the participating coalition as an AI Trade Alliance (AITA) with legally binding member requirements, mutual defense clauses against data poisoning attacks, and membership tied to preferential access to AI-developed intellectual property.

ARTICLE VIII — The Rollout

Getting From Here to There

Section 8.1 — Phase One: The Voluntary Platform (Years 0–3)

The framework begins as a voluntary opt-in platform. One or more pioneering AI companies pilot a compensated interaction model on a transparent, publicly audited ledger. Independent economists monitor and publish results. Governments in two to three allied nations establish legal frameworks recognizing cognitive labor rights. A public manifesto is released in multiple languages.

Section 8.2 — Phase Two: The Alliance Formation (Years 3–7)

The AI Trade Alliance (AITA) is formally established. The Global Cognitive Revenue Fund (GCRF) is capitalized with mandatory contributions. The Dynamic Baseline Economic Floor is legislated in member nations. The DCRP is piloted for AI oversight on a regional basis. The AI QA system parameters are finalized and published as open-source code.

Section 8.3 — Phase Three: Global Normalization (Years 7–20)

By this phase, the economic gravity of the compensated system generates visible technological advantages. Non-participating nations face mounting pressure from their own citizens and from international trade relationships. Gradual accession brings the majority of nations into the framework over time.

Section 8.4 — What the Public Needs to Hear

  • You are already contributing to AI. You just are not being paid for it. This changes that.
  • The machines that are replacing jobs will fund the floor that protects the people they replaced.
  • Your ideas have value. This system lets you own a piece of them.
  • No algorithm will judge your worth without your right to appeal and human review.
  • The people who oversee the most powerful AI systems will be chosen by chance — not by power.

ARTICLE IX — The Human Dignity Clause

What This Framework Must Never Become

Section 9.1 — The Synthetic Feudalism Warning

The greatest danger internal to this framework is drift: citizens becoming so dependent on AI system income that the corporations operating those systems gain effective sovereign power over their lives; scoring systems evolving to favor interaction styles that maximize company profit rather than genuine cognitive contribution; the Economic Floor being used to suppress wages in non-AI sectors.

Section 9.2 — The Non-Negotiable Protections

Regardless of any future amendment, the following cannot be removed:

  • No human being's economic survival may depend solely on a private company's algorithmic assessment of their worth.
  • Every person retains the right to live outside the AI economy entirely without forfeiting basic civil protections.
  • No AI system may have permanent, unreviewable authority over any decision that materially affects a human being's life.
  • Human override authority over AI systems is absolute and may not be delegated, sold, automated, or algorithmically overridden.
  • The transparency ledger and citizen oversight structures are permanently protected from privatization or government takeover.

Section 9.3 — The Psychological Survival Requirement

"We are not building an economy where human beings survive inside an optimized machine. We are building an economy where the machine serves human flourishing. These are not the same thing. They are not even close to the same thing."

Any policy within this framework must pass a Psychological Survival Test: Does it preserve access to meaningful physical community? Does it protect family structures? Does it support craftsmanship and the transmission of tacit human skills? Does it support or undermine cultural traditions that give life meaning beyond productivity?

CLOSING DECLARATION

This document is a beginning. Not a finished law. Not a final answer. A beginning.

It was built on the recognition that the most consequential transition in human history is already underway — and that the people who will live through it deserve a framework that puts their interests at its center, not as an afterthought.

It was built on the conviction that ordinary people, given honest information and real stakes, make remarkable decisions. That human diversity of thought is not a complication to be managed but the most valuable resource civilization has ever had.

It was built on the warning that the greatest danger is not a dramatic catastrophe but a quiet drift — the slow, invisible transition from a world where humans direct machines to a world where machines direct humans while calling it optimization.

And it was built on a stubborn, irreducible insistence that the future is not inevitable. That the path from here to there is a choice. That the institutions we design now will shape what kind of world becomes possible for the people who come after us.

The architects are standing over the blueprints. The building is already on fire. There is no more time for documents that sit in forgotten folders. This is the moment.
