The crisis

AI is advancing. Human rights are not.

For the first time in history, machines are learning from us — from our words, our creativity, our corrections, our culture. The people who provide this irreplaceable fuel receive nothing, while a small number of corporations accumulate unprecedented power and wealth. This is not a technology problem. It is a civilizational emergency.

The declaration

Human thought is economic infrastructure.

Every time a person interacts with an AI system, they create economic value. That value must be recognized, protected, and compensated. The Cognitive Age Initiative exists to embed this truth into the foundations of global law and governance.

Core principle: If your input trains AI, you are not a "user." You are a contributor. And contributors deserve compensation and rights.
The framework

What the World AI Constitution & Global Cognition Accord demand

Paid Cognitive Labor

Every qualifying human–AI interaction generates value. The framework establishes a paid-input economy where cognitive contribution is treated as labor, not exhaust.

Global Cognitive Revenue Fund

AI companies contribute a share of revenue into a global fund that supports economic stability, funds the floor, and reduces inequality.

Dynamic Economic Floor

No person falls below basic dignity as jobs are automated. A negative income tax model, funded by AI revenue, guarantees survival without killing initiative.

Transparent AI Governance

AI quality assessment systems must be open, auditable, and explainable in plain language — with real appeal paths for every person.

Idea Royalties

When human–AI collaboration produces commercially valuable ideas, contributors retain a licensing stake. A new class of "Idea Workers" emerges worldwide.

Humane Transition

Displaced workers receive guaranteed transition support: income, training, and pathways into the new cognitive economy — funded by the systems that displaced them.
