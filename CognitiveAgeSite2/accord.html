Global agreement

The Global Cognition Accord

The Global Cognition Accord is the world's first international agreement recognizing human cognitive contribution as economic value. It establishes the rights, protections, and compensation mechanisms required to ensure that every person benefits from the intelligence systems built on their contributions.

Where the World AI Constitution defines the principles, the Accord defines the implementation — the economic and structural commitments nations and institutions must adopt to protect humanity in the Cognitive Age.

Purpose

Why the Accord exists

AI systems are trained on the collective cognition of humanity. Every question asked, correction made, idea explored, and conversation held contributes to the intelligence of these systems. The Accord ensures that this contribution is not treated as free labor for private gain, but as a shared global resource that must be compensated and protected.

The Accord transforms human–AI interaction from invisible extraction into recognized economic participation.
Pillars of the Accord

The five commitments every nation must uphold

1. Cognitive Labor Recognition

Nations must legally recognize that human interaction with AI systems constitutes cognitive labor when it generates measurable economic value.

2. Global Cognitive Revenue Fund

A shared international fund financed by AI‑driven revenue, used to support economic stability, fund the dynamic floor, and reduce global inequality.

3. Dynamic Economic Floor

A guaranteed minimum standard of living funded by AI revenue, ensuring no person falls below basic dignity as automation expands.

4. Transparent AI Governance

Nations must adopt transparent, auditable AI oversight systems with clear appeal pathways for individuals affected by automated decisions.

5. Global Equity & Access

AI benefits must be accessible to all nations, not concentrated among a few. The Accord ensures equitable distribution of AI‑driven prosperity.

Economic structure

How the Accord works

The Accord establishes a global economic model where AI‑generated value is shared with the people whose cognition made that value possible.

  • AI companies contribute a percentage of revenue to the Global Cognitive Revenue Fund.
  • Nations receive allocations based on population, contribution, and economic need.
  • Funds support a dynamic economic floor, transition programs, and global equity initiatives.
  • All systems are independently audited to ensure transparency and fairness.

This model ensures that the Cognitive Age becomes a period of shared prosperity, not concentrated wealth.

Call to nations

A new global social contract

The Global Cognition Accord invites every nation to participate in a new economic framework built for the age of intelligence. It is not a treaty of competition, but of cooperation — a recognition that the future of AI must be shaped collectively, not left to market forces alone.

Nations that sign the Accord commit to protecting their citizens, strengthening their economies, and ensuring that AI becomes a tool of human advancement.
